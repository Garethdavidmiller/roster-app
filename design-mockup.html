What's different from the current design

1
Inter font. Replaces the system font. Designed for UI readability at small sizes — noticeably sharper on Android. One CDN link, nothing else to install.
2
Member avatar. Initials in a gold-rimmed circle confirm at a glance whose calendar you're viewing — without adding a new UI element, just repositioning the name that's already there.
3
Bolder month heading. Font weight 800, tracking tightened to −0.03em. The month name is the primary navigation landmark and should be read instantly without squinting.
4
Cell background tints. Each shift type applies a very subtle colour wash to the whole cell, not just the badge. Scroll past a week and you read the pattern — blue tiles, purple tiles, green tiles — before you read a single word.
5
Fully round pill badges + drop shadow. Border-radius 100px (fully round) instead of a small corner radius. A 1px 4px shadow lifts each badge off the cell surface. Small change, makes them look intentional.
6
Today cell — radial gradient. Gold radiates from the upper-right rather than a flat fill. Paired with a diffuse gold glow shadow. Today becomes unmistakable at a glance, which is the whole point of the today highlight.
7
Rounded cells with gap grid. Each cell has border-radius 8px. A 2px gap between cells (filled with the dark grid background colour) creates a subtle card-grid feel rather than a flat table.
8
Hover / tap feedback. Cells scale 5% on hover and 4% back on press — pure CSS, no JavaScript. On a desktop or tablet it makes the grid feel interactive. On Android it's invisible (touch events don't trigger :hover).
9
Nothing structural changed. Same seven-column layout, same badge labels and emojis, same brand colours, same legend, same summary strip. This is a visual polish pass only — zero logic changes.
