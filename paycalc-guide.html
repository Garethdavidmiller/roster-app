What it does

The Pay Calculator estimates your net take-home pay for any 4-week pay period. You enter your hours manually — the calculator applies your tax code, pension, and any extras to give you a breakdown of gross pay, deductions, and net. It is an estimate only — your payslip is the authoritative figure.

Privacy: All figures are saved on your device only — nothing is sent to any server or visible to anyone else.

First-time setup

  1. Open the Pay Calculator via the 💷 Pay button on the roster
  2. Tap Set up your pay calculator if the settings card is visible
  3. Enter your hourly rate (check your latest payslip)
  4. Enter your tax code (e.g. 1257L) — find it on your payslip or P60
  5. Enter your pension amount — the £ figure shown as "Smart RPS CR Scheme" on your payslip
  6. If you have a student loan, choose your plan
  7. Tap Save settings

Choosing a pay period

Use the dropdown or the ← Prev / Next → buttons to navigate between 4-week pay periods. The current period is highlighted. Payday for each period is shown in the period label.

Pay cut-off: The ✂️ icon on the roster calendar marks the Saturday before payday — changes entered after that date count towards the next period.

Entering your hours

Your standard contracted hours (Mon–Fri shifts) are included automatically — you only need to enter hours that attract a different pay rate:

  • Rostered Saturdays — 1.25× rate
  • Overtime and rest day working — 1.25× rate
  • Sundays — 1.5× rate
  • Bank holidays (rostered) — 1.25× rate (row appears automatically)
  • Boxing Day (26 Dec) — 3× rate (row appears automatically)
Fill blanks from roster → If your name appears in the roster, a hint bar shows how many Saturdays, Sundays, and bank holidays fall in this period. Tap Fill blanks from roster → to pre-fill any empty fields from your base roster in one tap. It only fills fields you haven't already entered — it will never overwrite hours you've typed in yourself. Filled fields turn gold; the highlight clears as soon as you edit them. Base roster only — swaps or changes you've already requested won't be reflected.

Holiday Pay Premium (HPP)

HPP is a portion of your irregular pay (RDW, overtime, Sunday, BH premiums) set aside so that your holiday pay reflects what you actually earn — not just your basic rate.

It accumulates throughout the year and is paid as a lump sum in January. The calculator tracks your running HPP total and shows what you have accrued so far.

Why is it shown but not in the net figure? Because you don't receive it until January. The accrual figure is shown separately so you can see it building — it won't appear in your monthly take-home.

Improving accuracy — YTD figures

Tax is calculated incrementally across the year. To get a sharper estimate, open the Improve Accuracy section and enter two figures from the Year to Date box on your latest payslip:

  • Total taxable pay this year — "Taxable Pay" from your payslip's Year to Date box
  • Total tax deducted this year — "Tax Paid" from your payslip's Year to Date box

These figures let the calculator run a running-total tax estimate rather than guessing from scratch each period.

Pay Rise Back Pay

When a pay award is backdated to April, expand the Pay Rise Back Pay card. Enter your old and new hourly rates (and London Allowance if it changed) and the calculator works out the lump sum owed across all periods since the award start date.

Data and privacy

Settings and YTD figures are saved in your browser's localStorage — they persist between sessions but stay on your device only. Clearing your browser data will remove them. Nothing is uploaded to any server.

Pension is saved per pay period. If your pension amount changes mid-year, update the field in Settings — the new amount is saved from that point forward and past periods automatically keep their own recorded amount. The label next to the pension field shows which period you are currently editing.
