What you get

🃏

FIP Card

Discounted fares on most European railways. The standard discount is 50%; French, Belgian and Irish trains offer 75% off. The card lasts 2 years. Buy tickets at station offices abroad, or book from the UK via Trainseurope or bookmyrst.co.uk.

🎟️

Free Coupons

Free travel on participating train operators. On some trains you'll also need to pay for a seat reservation or a supplement. Each coupon covers 48 hours in one country. You get a new set every year.

Who's covered Covers you, your partner (you must live together) and your dependent children. Whether family members get free coupons depends on the country — check the country cards below. Confirm with RST before ordering coupons for family members.
Family coupons — not every country Where family coupons aren't available, your partner and children can still travel at the FIP discount rate using the FIP Card.

How it works

  1. Apply for your FIP Card Use the RST form — link at the bottom of this page. Allow at least 6 weeks. Apply now even if you have no travel plans — if you wait until you need it, you'll be stuck waiting. When it arrives, sign it and check your name matches your passport.
  2. Order your free coupons once a year Use the RST coupon form (link at the bottom). Order at least 3 weeks before travel — but no more than 3 months ahead, as they expire 3 months from the date of issue. You get one coupon per country each year. France, Belgium, the Netherlands and Ireland give you two per year — plus two for each eligible family member.
  3. Understand how a coupon works Each coupon has 4 dated boxes. When you write a date in a box, you get 48 hours of free travel on participating operators in that country — from midnight at the start of that day through to midnight at the end of the next. So one coupon = up to 4 separate 48-hour trips in that country.
  4. Book your trip For most countries you can walk up to the ticket office once you arrive. To book ahead from the UK, your best options are Trainseurope (call 01354 660222 — you speak to a person and get it sorted) or bookmyrst.co.uk (online request form — allow at least 3 weeks, they email you a quote). See the "Booking from the UK" section below.
  5. Travel with card and passport Always carry both together. Staff at the ticket office and on board may ask to see them.
⚠ Filling in your coupons — permanent ink only Use a permanent ballpoint pen. Any crossing out or correction invalidates the coupon — it will be refused at the ticket office. Write carefully and check before you hand them over.
⚠ On day 2 of your 48 hours — watch the inspector Some inspectors try to mark a second box on day 2, thinking each day needs a new date. It doesn't — one date covers a full 48 hours (write Monday's date and you're valid for all of Monday and all of Tuesday). Politely point this out if it happens.

European destinations

Tap a country to see what to expect and how to book.

Always compare public fares first A FIP discounted fare is not automatically the cheapest option. Advance public tickets, promotions, and low-cost operators can sometimes beat the FIP price — especially outside peak periods. Check before you book.
ℹ️ Travelling through more than one country? ▾

You need a coupon for each country you pass through — even if it's one continuous train.

Example: the Vienna–Venice overnight crosses from Austria into Italy at the border. You need one ÖBB (Austria) coupon and one Trenitalia (Italy) coupon for that single journey. The Belfast–Dublin Enterprise is the same — one NIR coupon for the Northern Ireland section, one Irish Rail coupon for the Republic of Ireland section.

The exact changeover point between coupons is set per route. Trainseurope can tell you exactly which coupons you need before you order them.

Note: Eurostar and Eurostar Red don't accept coupons at all — fixed FIP fares apply instead (see sections above).

🇫🇷 France ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff + family ✓

Operator: SNCF (including TGV high-speed trains)

Show your FIP Card at an SNCF ticket office for 75% off most fares. Free coupons are also accepted. Reservations are compulsory on TGV and other long-distance trains. With the 75% FIP ticket the reservation is included in the price; if you're using a free coupon, a small reservation fee applies on top.

Some international TGV routes use a set fare rather than 75% off — and free coupons may not apply. The Paris–Switzerland TGV (Lyria) is the most common example: coupons are not valid, and a special FIP fare applies instead. Always check before booking any international route.

Domestic French journeys must be booked at an SNCF station ticket office or by phone — not online. From the UK, call the SNCF Contact Centre on 00 33 1 84 94 3635.

Getting around Paris The Paris Métro is not covered by FIP — buy a regular ticket or day pass. The RER is a mix: some lines are SNCF-operated (FIP valid) and some are RATP-operated (not valid). The simplest approach when you're in the city is to use a standard ticket — it's not worth the hassle of working out which section you're on.
⚠ OUIGO (low-cost TGV) — FIP not valid OUIGO trains will not accept FIP tickets or coupons.
🇧🇪 Belgium ⚠ buy before boarding from Jul 2026▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff + family ✓

Operator: SNCB / NMBS

Both 75% FIP tickets and free coupons are valid. No reservation is required — buy your ticket and travel.

⚠ From 1 July 2026 — buy before boarding SNCB will no longer sell tickets on board, including FIP discounted tickets. Buy at a station ticket desk before you board. Do not assume you can pay on the train.
🇳🇱 Netherlands ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff + family ✓

Operator: NS

FIP tickets and coupons are bought at NS International ticket counters. Known locations include Amsterdam Centraal, Rotterdam Centraal, Schiphol Airport and Utrecht Centraal. Check which counters are open before you travel — availability can vary.

ICE trains: A €3 supplement applies per journey if you're using a 50% FIP ticket on an ICE high-speed train. This charge doesn't apply if you're using a free coupon.
🇮🇪 Republic of Ireland & Northern Ireland ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff + family ✓

Republic of Ireland: Iarnród Éireann (Irish Rail)
FIP gives 75% off or free travel (with coupons) on all Irish Rail mainline services. Buy tickets at Irish Rail station offices. No high-speed supplements — just the discounted fare.

Northern Ireland: Translink / NIR
FIP gives 75% off or free travel (with coupons) on NIR services. Buy tickets at Translink station offices.

Belfast ↔ Dublin — Enterprise service The cross-border Enterprise train is jointly operated by NIR and Iarnród Éireann. You need two coupons — one NIR coupon (valid Belfast to the border) and one Iarnród Éireann coupon (valid from the border to Dublin). Despite this, you travel the full journey on one train without changing — the two coupons simply cover each operator's section.
🇩🇪 Germany ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff only

Operator: DB (Deutsche Bahn)

Buy at any DB station ticket office, or book ahead at bahn.de. ICE high-speed trains are included, though a supplement applies on top. Free coupons are valid for staff.

Cross-border routes such as Paris–Frankfurt (TGV/ICE) can also be booked at DB ticket offices.

🇦🇹 Austria ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff + family ✓

Operator: ÖBB

Both 50% FIP tickets and free coupons are valid on ÖBB trains, including the flagship Railjet high-speed service. No supplement applies on top of the 50% FIP ticket — you just pay half the fare. Buy at any ÖBB ticket office or ticket machine.

Railjet class: A second-class coupon covers Economy class; first-class coupons are needed for Railjet first class. Business class on Railjet isn't bookable with FIP at all.
🇮🇹 Italy ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff + family ✓

Operator: Trenitalia

Both 50% FIP tickets and free coupons are accepted at Trenitalia ticket offices. On high-speed Frecciarossa and Frecciargento trains, a seat reservation is required — the fee is paid on top of the discounted fare.

⚠ Italo (private operator) — FIP not valid The Italo high-speed trains are a private company and do not accept FIP.
🇪🇸 Spain ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff only

Operator: Renfe

Staff can use free coupons on standard Renfe services. On AVE high-speed trains a fixed staff fare applies (from €15) rather than 50% off — buy this at a Renfe ticket office in Spain, not from the UK in advance.

Euskotren (Basque Country) — free travel Euskotren is a separate rail network in the Basque Country (Bilbao and San Sebastián) that has joined FIP — and travel is completely free. No coupon needed, just show your FIP Card. Staff can also use coupons.
⚠ Ouigo Spain — FIP not valid Like OUIGO in France, the low-cost Ouigo trains in Spain don't accept FIP.
🇨🇭 Switzerland ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff only

Operators: SBB (Swiss Federal Railways), BLS, and most Swiss private railways

FIP covers almost the entire Swiss rail network — SBB, BLS, and a consortium of private railways all take part. FIP gives 50% off throughout the network; free coupons are valid for staff. Buy at any SBB or BLS station ticket counter, online at sbb.ch, or from the UK via Trainseurope.

Mountain tourist railways — check first Some scenic mountain railways and cable cars (mainly around Grindelwald and Mürren) are not in FIP. If you're planning a specific mountain route, confirm with RDG or Trainseurope before you go.
Travelling to Italy — supplement applies Cross-border EC/ECE trains to Milan carry a €20 supplement per journey on top of your FIP ticket or coupon. If you book via DB (German railways), the supplement is lower (€10–13) and includes a seat reservation.
⚠ Paris–Switzerland TGV (Lyria) — coupons not valid These cross-border high-speed trains use a special FIP fare rather than 50% off, and free coupons are not accepted. Book via Trainseurope or SNCF direct.
🇵🇹 Portugal ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff only

Operator: CP (Comboios de Portugal)

50% FIP tickets and free coupons are valid on CP services. Coupons are for staff only — family members travel on the 50% FIP Card. Buy at any CP station ticket desk, or book from the UK via Trainseurope. Covers all main routes including Lisbon–Porto and Lisbon–Faro.

On long-distance AP (Alfa Pendular) and IC (Intercidades) trains, a seat reservation is required — this is booked alongside your FIP ticket at the ticket desk.

Vigo–Porto (Celta service) The cross-border Celta train between Vigo (Spain) and Porto uses a special FIP fare for the entire route. Ask at the ticket office — both a CP coupon and Renfe ticket together don't cover it; the fixed Celta FIP fare does.
⚠ Fertagus — FIP not valid Fertagus runs the R line from Lisbon (Roma-Areeiro) south towards Setúbal and is a private operator not in FIP. If you're travelling in the Lisbon area and see a train labelled "Setúbal" on Google Maps, check it's a CP service before assuming FIP applies.
🇱🇺 Luxembourg — free for everyone ▾
Free for all

Operator: CFL

Luxembourg is unique — domestic travel in second class is completely free for everyone. Trains, trams and buses. You don't need a ticket or your FIP Card to travel in second class — just board and go.

First class: A free coupon or 50% FIP ticket is required for first class. Second class is always free.
🇩🇰 Denmark ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff only

Operator: DSB

Both 50% FIP tickets and free coupons are valid on DSB main-line trains — coupons are for staff only. Buy at DSB ticket offices. The Hamburg–Copenhagen EC is a popular route running several times daily — book ahead as it fills up.

Branch lines — private operators Some branch lines run by GoCollective, Lokaltog, and other private operators are not in FIP. Stick to the DSB network or check before you travel.
⚠ Night trains and Copenhagen–Sweden — FIP not valid Night trains passing through Denmark (SJ and Snälltåget) don't accept FIP. Trains from Copenhagen towards Sweden (Malmö and beyond) use the Øresund bridge via Øresundståg — also not in FIP. You'll need a regular ticket for both.
🇳🇴 Norway ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff only

Main FIP operator: Vy (Norwegian national railway)

Both 50% FIP tickets and free coupons are valid on Vy trains — coupons are for staff only. Buy at Vy ticket offices. SJ Nord and Go-Ahead Nordic may also accept FIP — check with RDG before travel to confirm.

⚠ Airport Express (Flytoget) — FIP not valid The airport express to Oslo Gardermoen is run by a private operator and does not accept FIP. Use a regular ticket or the normal Vy service instead.
🇨🇿 Czech Republic ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff only

Operator: České dráhy (ČD)

FIP covers most ČD routes, but not all — a small number of ČD services are exceptions, and some trains carry a supplement. When using coupons, verify your specific journey before you go. When buying a FIP 50 ticket at the ČD desk, staff will only issue it for valid trains, so that's the safer option. Buy at ČD ticket offices. Prague is well connected to Vienna, Berlin, and Budapest — cross-border journeys need a FIP ticket from each country's operator.

⚠ Private operators — FIP not valid Several private operators share Czech routes but don't accept FIP: RegioJet, Leo Express, ARRIVA vlaky, GW Train Regio, and Trilex/Die Länderbahn. Stick to ČD trains — and check before you travel, as private operators run alongside ČD on many corridors.
🇭🇺 Hungary ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff only

Operator: MÁV (Hungarian State Railways)

Both 50% FIP tickets and free coupons are valid on MÁV services — coupons for staff only. Buy at MÁV ticket offices. Budapest is the main hub and well connected to Vienna by Railjet (around 2h 40min) — note that the Railjet is ÖBB-operated, so an Austrian coupon covers that journey.

🇭🇷 Croatia ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff only

Operator: HŽ (Croatian Railways)

Both 50% FIP tickets and free coupons are valid on HŽ services — coupons for staff only. Buy at HŽ ticket offices on arrival, or book from the UK via Trainseurope (route options are limited).

Limited coastal rail network Most popular Croatian destinations — Dubrovnik, Split, Hvar — are not well served by rail. Zagreb is the main hub and useful for connections to Ljubljana and Budapest, but reaching the coast requires a bus or ferry from Zagreb.
🇬🇷 Greece ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff + family ✓

Operator: Hellenic Train

Both 50% FIP tickets and free coupons are valid on Hellenic Train services. Buy at Hellenic Train ticket offices. The main intercity route is Athens–Thessaloniki.

Getting around Athens The Athens Metro is run by a separate company and is not covered by FIP — buy a standard metro ticket for travel within the city.
🇵🇱 Poland ▾
FIP Card ✓ Coupons: staff only KMŁ new April 2026

Operators: PKP (Polish State Railways) and regional operators including KMŁ

Both 50% FIP tickets and free coupons are valid on PKP services — coupons for staff only. Buy at PKP ticket offices.

KMŁ (Koleje Małopolskie) — joined April 2026 KMŁ regional services in southern Poland (including routes around Kraków) joined FIP in April 2026. Buy at KMŁ ticket offices.
🇸🇪 Sweden FIP not valid

SJ (the main operator) is not in FIP — pay standard public fares.

Ferry services

A small number of ferry operators take part in FIP — useful for reaching Ireland or Greece, whether you're travelling as a foot passenger or taking a car.

⛴️ Stena Line — North Sea (Harwich–Hook of Holland) ▾
Coupons: staff + family ✓

Route: Harwich → Hook of Holland (Netherlands)

Free travel with coupons — you need two coupons per person (staff and each eligible family member). A Port Tax and Environmental Charge is payable separately — not covered by the coupon.

You must book in advance — tickets cannot be bought on board. Call Stena on 01255 202352 and have your coupon number(s) ready before you call.

Overnight crossing: A cabin must be booked — it's not optional. Do this at the same time as booking your ticket.

Always check RDG before booking — ferry FIP details can change.

⛴️ Stena Line — Irish Sea ▾
FIP Card: 50% at port only No coupons

Routes: Holyhead → Dublin  |  Fishguard → Rosslare  |  Cairnryan → Belfast

Coupons are not accepted on Irish Sea Stena routes. A 50% FIP discount is available, but only when buying in person at the port ticket office on the day of travel — advance booking on a FIP fare is not possible from the UK.

Always check RDG before booking — ferry FIP details can change.

⛴️ Superfast Ferries — Italy to Greece ▾
Fixed fare — no coupons

Routes: Ancona / Bari → Patras, Igoumenitsa, Corfu

Superfast Ferries (operated by Attica Group) use fixed FIP Global Fares — not a percentage discount and not standard coupons. You need to book by sending the Attica FIP Reservation Form by email before you travel. The current booking contact and form are available on the full RDG FIP guide (link at the bottom of this page).

Always check RDG for current contact details before booking — these arrangements can change.

Booking from the UK

Four ways to book your FIP trip from the UK.

  1. Trainseurope Call 01354 660222 — Mon–Fri 09:00–19:00, Sat–Sun 10:00–15:00. Speak to a person and get it booked in one call. Handles most countries.
  2. SNCF direct Call 00 33 1 84 94 3635 — ask for the English option. Best for Eurostar and French-operated trains. They can email tickets to you.
  3. bookmyrst.co.uk Online request form — submit your journey and they email a quote. Allow at least 3 weeks — no telephone bookings. Covers most routes.
  4. Ffestiniog Travel A specialist rail agency. Email rail@ffestiniogtravel.com — worth trying if Trainseurope can't help with your route.

Common mistakes

  • Assuming Eurostar accepts FIP coupons — it doesn't. Fixed FIP fares apply instead
  • Assuming FIP is always cheaper than public fares — compare advance tickets first
  • Not booking FIP seats early — they're limited and sell out weeks ahead on popular routes
  • Writing on a coupon too early — fill in the date as close to travel as possible, in permanent ink, no corrections
  • Using coupons on private or low-cost operators (Italo, OUIGO, Ouigo Spain, RegioJet) — these don't accept FIP
  • Assuming city metros and trams are included — they almost never are
  • Forgetting that reservations are still required on many high-speed trains even with a coupon
  • Trying to buy on board in Belgium from July 2026 — you must buy before boarding

What to carry

  • Passport
  • FIP Card (if using discounted fares)
  • FIP coupons filled in correctly (if using free travel)
  • Seat reservation or supplement confirmation where required
  • Printed or digital ticket confirmation
  • Any booking reference from Trainseurope / bookmyrst.co.uk

Important to know

  • Leisure travel only — not for commuting or any work purpose
  • Always carry your FIP Card and passport together
  • Book early — FIP seats are limited and sell out fast on popular trains
  • Eligible for you, your spouse/partner, and dependent children
  • Participating operators only — city metros, trams, buses and private rail operators are generally not in FIP. You'll need a regular ticket to get around each city
  • Coupons match your UK staff travel pass class — if you have a standard (second-class) pass, your coupons are for second class
  • FIP is not a contractual entitlement — it can change or be withdrawn at any time
  • Normal delay and cancellation terms apply — travel is at your own risk
