🇪🇺 FIP Travel

European travel for Chiltern Railways staff

What you get

🃏

FIP Card

Half price on most European train fares — buy tickets at station offices

🎟️

Free Coupons

A small annual allowance of completely free journeys on partner railways

Both cover you, your spouse or partner, and dependent children.

How it works

  1. Apply for your FIP Card A physical card you apply for once — valid for 2 years. Sign it, and make sure your name matches your passport.
  2. Order your free coupons once a year Fill in the online form at least 3 weeks before you travel. Once you have them, fill them in using a permanent ballpoint pen — no corrections allowed.
  3. Book your trip Each country works slightly differently. Most require you to buy a ticket at a station ticket office rather than online. See the country guides below.
  4. Travel with card and passport Always carry both together. Staff at the ticket office and on board may ask to see them.
⚠ Filling in your coupons — permanent ink only Use a permanent ballpoint pen. Any crossing out or correction invalidates the coupon — it will be refused at the ticket office. Write carefully and check before you hand them over.

European destinations

Tap a country to see what to expect and how to book.

🇫🇷 France ▾
FIP Card ✓ Free Coupons ✓

Operator: SNCF (including TGV high-speed trains)

Show your FIP Card at an SNCF ticket office for 50% off most fares. Free coupons are also accepted. Reservations are compulsory on TGV and long-distance Intercités trains — there's a booking fee on top of the discounted fare.

Domestic France journeys must be bought at a ticket office (by phone or in person) — not online.

Getting around Paris The Paris Métro is not covered by FIP — buy a regular ticket or day pass. The RER is a mix: some lines are SNCF-operated (FIP valid) and some are RATP-operated (not valid). The simplest approach when you're in the city is to use a standard ticket — it's not worth the hassle of working out which section you're on.
⚠ OUIGO (low-cost TGV) — FIP not valid OUIGO trains will not accept FIP tickets or coupons.
🇧🇪 Belgium ▾
FIP Card ✓ Free Coupons ✓

Operator: SNCB / NMBS

Both 50% FIP tickets and free coupons are valid. No reservation is required — buy your ticket and travel.

From July 2026: Tickets cannot be bought on board. Make sure you buy before boarding at a station ticket desk.
🇳🇱 Netherlands ▾
FIP Card ✓ Free Coupons ✓

Operator: NS

FIP tickets and coupons must currently be bought at the NS International counter at Amsterdam Centraal (western entrance, near the taxi rank) — the only counter in the Netherlands offering this service.

ICE trains: A €3 supplement applies per journey if you're using a 50% FIP ticket on an ICE high-speed train. This charge doesn't apply if you're using a free coupon.
🇩🇪 Germany ▾
FIP Card ✓ Free Coupons ✓

Operator: DB (Deutsche Bahn)

Buy your 50% ticket at any DB Reisezentrum (station ticket office). You can also now book online at bahn.de — good for planning ahead before you travel. ICE high-speed trains are included, though a supplement applies.

Cross-border routes such as Paris–Frankfurt (TGV/ICE) can also be booked at DB ticket offices.

🇮🇹 Italy ▾
FIP Card ✓ Free Coupons ✓

Operator: Trenitalia

FIP 50% tickets are available at Trenitalia ticket offices. On Frecciarossa and Frecciargento high-speed services, a reservation is mandatory — the reservation fee is paid on top of the discounted fare.

⚠ Italo (private operator) — FIP not valid The Italo high-speed trains are a private company and do not accept FIP.
🇪🇸 Spain ▾
FIP Card ✓

Operator: Renfe

A FIP Global Fare is available for AVE high-speed trains from €15. Tickets must be bought at a Renfe ticket office in Spain — you can't book these online in advance from the UK.

⚠ Ouigo Spain — FIP not valid Like OUIGO in France, the low-cost Ouigo trains in Spain don't accept FIP.
🇨🇭 Switzerland ▾
FIP Card ✓ Free Coupons ✓

Operator: SBB CFF FFS

FIP gives 50% off throughout Switzerland on SBB trains. Buy tickets at SBB ticket counters or on the SBB website. Some cross-border services may carry supplements — check at the counter when booking.

🇮🇪 Republic of Ireland & Northern Ireland ▾
FIP Card ✓ Free Coupons ✓

Republic of Ireland: Iarnród Éireann (Irish Rail)
FIP gives 50% off or free travel (with coupons) on all Irish Rail mainline services. Buy tickets at Irish Rail station offices. No high-speed supplements — just the discounted fare.

Northern Ireland: Translink / NIR
FIP coupons are valid on Northern Ireland Railways services. Buy tickets at Translink station offices.

Belfast ↔ Dublin — Enterprise service The cross-border Enterprise train is jointly operated by NIR and Iarnród Éireann. You need two coupons — one NIR coupon (valid Belfast to the border) and one Iarnród Éireann coupon (valid from the border to Dublin). Despite this, you travel the full journey on one train without changing — the two coupons simply cover each operator's section.

Ferry services

A small number of ferry operators take part in FIP — handy for reaching Ireland, the Netherlands, or Greece, especially if you're taking a car.

⛴️ Stena Line — North Sea & Irish Sea ▾
Free Coupons ✓

North Sea: Harwich → Hook of Holland (Netherlands)

Irish Sea: Holyhead → Dublin  |  Fishguard → Rosslare  |  Cairnryan → Belfast

Your FIP coupon covers the basic crossing fare on all of these routes. You'll also need to pay a Port Tax and Environmental Charge separately — these are not covered by the coupon.

You must book in advance — tickets cannot be bought on board. Call Stena on 01255 202352 and have your coupon number(s) ready before you call.

Overnight crossings: A cabin must be booked — it's not optional. Do this at the same time as booking your ticket.
⛴️ Superfast Ferries — Italy to Greece ▾
Free Coupons ✓

Routes: Ancona / Bari → Patras, Igoumenitsa, Corfu

FIP coupons are valid. You need to book by sending the Attica FIP Reservation Form by email before you travel. The current booking contact and form are available on the full RDG FIP guide (link at the bottom of this page).

Important to know

  • Leisure travel only — not for commuting or any work purpose
  • Always carry your FIP Card and passport together
  • Book early — seats are quota-controlled and limited
  • Eligible for you, your spouse/partner, and dependent children
  • National trains only — city metros, trams and buses are run by separate companies not in FIP. You'll need a regular ticket to get around each city
  • FIP is not a contractual entitlement — it can change or be withdrawn at any time
  • Normal delay and cancellation terms apply — travel is at your own risk
